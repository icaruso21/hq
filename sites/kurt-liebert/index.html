About Kurt

When he was a kid, Kurt Liebert wanted to be a rockstar. So he started writing music. In 1994, he put together a band called Bicycle — they biked across the entire continental US from New York City to Seattle, playing gigs along the way, landing a story in The New York Times Magazine, and recording an EP at Prince's Paisley Park studios.

Their album on Capricorn Records made the Huffington Post's list of the most underrated albums of the 1990s.

In 2001, Kurt broke up the band and started painting. Acrylic on plywood. Found objects as canvases. Every piece is one of a kind — raw, irreverent, and rooted in a life lived on the road and in the rain of the Pacific Northwest.

"Painting is so much different than writing songs. Painters don't ever have to paint the same painting twice."
